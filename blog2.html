TYPES OF INVESTORS

Who are investors?

An investor is an individual, corporation, or financial firm that invests its financial capital in a particular instrument, currency, or company in hopes of achieving similar financial returns in the future. Corporations can be investors, and even mutual funds can be called investors. Investors invest through various markets and financial channels that help direct their capital toward a single goal so that they can create additional wealth over time. Investors invest their money for many reasons. For some it's a job, for some it's a passion and for others it's just a means of earning additional income over time that can help them in their old age.

Investors have different risk tolerances, capitals, styles, preferences and timeframes. For example, some investors prefer very low-risk investments that generate conservative returns, such as certificates of deposit and certain fixed income products. However, other investors tend to take additional risks to achieve greater returns. These investors can invest in currencies, emerging markets, or stocks. A distinction can be made between the terms "investor" and "trader". This is because investors typically hold positions for years or even decades, while traders typically hold positions for a shorter period of time. For example, a scalp trader only holds a position for a few seconds.

Before going deep into types of investors, investors are categorised into two major types.

1.Active Investors

2.Passive Investors

1.Active Investor: Active investor is the one who regularly invests and investing is the integral part of his/her life. Cryptocurrency investors, stock market investors can be categorised into active investors.

2.Passive Investor: These are those investors who invest with the perspective of long term returns. They may not get an appropriate return at the initial stage but hold a strong position in the long term.Real Estate investors, mutual funds investors can be categorised into passive investors.

Now we will be discussing various types of investors. There are basically 5 types of investors:

1.Angel Investors

An angel investor is an investor who has amassed a great deal of wealth and income for himself. These investors earn three to four times earnings, or more, than the most successful average man earns. Their net worths often reach into the millions and these investors can be found anywhere across the industry. Angel investors primarily invest in early-stage companies and startups by buying large amounts of equity. This investment usually takes the form of either a loan or a stock purchase. For example, some angel investors specialize in a particular type of business, such as new technology. In some cases, these angels make high-risk investments in hopes of obtaining large returns if the company is acquired by a larger company or goes public on the stock exchange. This type of angel investor is usually most active when the economy is strong or stable.

2.Personal Investor

An individual investor is an individual investor who invests capital in a company or other investment opportunity for personal gain. If these types of investors want to invest in a company, they must go through a rigorous documented process in order to do so.

3.Venture Capitalist

A is a private equity investor who provides capital to companies with high growth potential in exchange for participation in the equity. They are typically deployed when a large amount of money has been invested and the business has shown significant earnings potential. If you are working with a venture capitalist, consider drafting a detailed partnership agreement outlining the rights and expectations of each party. Venture capitalists sometimes play a mentoring role, but are usually more active in the business. When looking for venture capital opportunities, it's best to get recommendations from mutual acquaintances. For example, ask an existing investor to share your business with their network for even greater funding opportunities.

4.Banks

Banks are the classic source of corporate credit. You must provide proof of your source of income or collateral before your application is approved. For this reason, banks are usually the better option for established businesses, but you don't have to be a big shot to get the funding.

5.Peer-to-Peer

Lending Peer-to-peer lending allows start-ups and entrepreneurs to create online profiles of their projects. These services seek to connect small business owners and entrepreneurs and cut out the middleman. SBA encourages startups interested in peer-to-peer lending to develop a business plan and company history outlining their progress and key achievements, as well as the results of market research, industry analysis, and financial projections. The goal is to show potential lenders that your business has what it takes to make money and thrive. In some cases, lenders will ask you to take steps to improve your credit score before receiving credit approval. It is important to understand the terms of the loan and avoid delinquency. Businesses should also be familiar with government regulations regarding peer-to-peer lending.

Conclusion

Investors are an integral part of the business world and a basic understanding of investors and how they work is essential. From the above article we can conclude that majorly there are 5 types of investors and are broadly categorised into two major types i.e active and passive investors
